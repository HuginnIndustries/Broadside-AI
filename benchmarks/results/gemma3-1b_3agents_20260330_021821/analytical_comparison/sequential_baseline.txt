**Detailed Breakdown by Use Case for 10M Rows:**

* **Query Speed (High):**  For 10M rows, *SQLite* and *PostgreSQL* are likely to provide faster query execution, particularly for simple queries. DuckDB offers a competitive performance, but its focus on performance could lead to slightly longer query times for more complex operations.

* **Ecosystem Maturity (Moderate):** PostgreSQL is currently the most mature and has the largest community, extensive tooling, and migration options.  SQLite and DuckDB are rapidly growing but have less widespread adoption.

**Recommendation for your 10M Row Analytics:**

1. **If Ease of Use and Quick Setup are Paramount:** Start with **SQLite**. It’s perfect for proof-of-concept projects and if you're comfortable with a simpler, self-contained solution.
2. **If you need High Performance for complex queries (especially for data types and joins):** Choose **PostgreSQL**. It offers a balance of performance and features that will handle the query load of a 10M row dataset.
3. **If you need efficient, low-level data processing, and a lightweight engine:** **DuckDB** is a strong contender. It shines in specific analytical scenarios where memory usage is extremely important.

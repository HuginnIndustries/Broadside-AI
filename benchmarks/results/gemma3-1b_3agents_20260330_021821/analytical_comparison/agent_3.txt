**Recommendations for Your 10M Row Analytics Workload:**

* **SQLite:**  If your data is relatively simple, the volume of reads is high, and you're focused on simple reporting, SQLite is an excellent starting point.  It's excellent for embedded systems or scenarios where you don’t require complex analytics.
* **PostgreSQL:**  If you anticipate more complex queries, data integrity is a priority, or you plan to expand your data model, PostgreSQL is the better choice.  It's a robust and versatile database.
* **DuckDB:**  If you are working with large datasets, have specific data types that are difficult to represent in a standard SQL database, or need extremely optimized query performance, DuckDB is a strong contender.  It's optimized for fast analytical processing.


**Important Considerations:**

* **Data Volume & Complexity:** The specific needs of your data will drive the choice.
* **Query Patterns:** Understand your typical query types.  Simple queries won’t benefit from PostgreSQL’s advanced features.
* **Scalability Requirements:** If you anticipate growth beyond 10 million rows, PostgreSQL's mature ecosystem provides better scalability.

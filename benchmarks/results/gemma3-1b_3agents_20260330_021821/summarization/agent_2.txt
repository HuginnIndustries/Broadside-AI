Okay, here's a summarized opinionated take on microservices vs. monolith for a 5-engineer B2B SaaS startup, aiming for under 200 words:

**For a startup like yours, microservices offer potential, but are a risky gamble.** While offering scalability and independent deployments, it’s *significantly more complex* to manage.

**Monolith wins in simplicity and quicker initial release.** It’s easier to build, test, and understand.  Five engineers can manage it effectively – at least initially.

**However, microservices are likely overkill.**  You’ll face a massive overhead in infrastructure, tooling, monitoring, and communication between services.  

**Recommendation:** Start with a monolith. It’s simpler, faster to launch, and provides a solid foundation. As you mature and need to scale, *then* consider microservices for specific, independent components.  Don’t over-engineer; prioritize core functionality.  **Focus on delivering value quickly – a simpler, more manageable architecture is better in the long run.**

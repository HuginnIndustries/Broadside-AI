Okay, for a B2B SaaS startup with 5 engineers, **microservices are generally the better long-term choice, but it’s a significant shift.**

**Pros of Microservices:**

* **Scalability:** Easier to scale individual services based on demand – crucial for growth.
* **Flexibility & Innovation:** Smaller teams can iterate faster and experiment with new features.
* **Technology Diversity:** Allows you to choose the best tools for each service.


**Cons of Microservices:**

* **Complexity:** Increased operational overhead – requires robust monitoring, deployment, and tooling.
* **Cost:** More infrastructure and management are needed. 
* **Coordination Challenges:** Distributed teams demand more communication and coordination.

**Monolith is fine initially,** but it quickly becomes brittle, difficult to maintain, and limiting as your team grows.  **Focus on developing a strong, well-defined microservice architecture initially.**


**Ultimately, prioritize clear, independent services that solve specific business problems.**  Don’t over-engineer.


---

**Disclaimer:** This is a general recommendation and depends heavily on the specific product and team.

Imagine a developer’s workflow where every meaningful git commit is instantly transformed into a polished, categorized, and audience-ready changelog entry — no more tedious manual drafting, inconsistent formatting, or missed updates. **ChangelogGen** analyzes your commit history using semantic intent detection (not just keywords), automatically classifying changes as “Feature,” “Fix,” “Breaking,” or “Refactor,” mapping them to semantic versioning rules, and generating clean, Markdown-formatted entries tailored for GitHub Releases, npm, or internal docs — all with zero configuration. It respects conventional commits, understands co-authored changes, ignores noise like “wip” or “typo,” and even suggests upgrade notes based on breaking changes detected in your code. Whether you’re shipping a solo open-source library or coordinating a 50-person engineering team, ChangelogGen turns your git log from a debugging artifact into your product’s most trusted communication channel — saving hours every release cycle while ensuring users and stakeholders always know exactly what changed, why it matters, and what they need to do. No more guesswork. No more delays. Just clear, accurate, changelogs — generated as you code.

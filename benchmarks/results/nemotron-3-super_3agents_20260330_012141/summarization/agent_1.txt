For a 5-engineer startup building a B2B SaaS product, **monolith architecture is the clear, opinionated winner** — and microservices are almost certainly a premature optimization.

**Monolith Pros:**  
- **Velocity:** One codebase, one deploy, minimal DevOps overhead. With 5 engineers, you can ship features fast without context-switching between services.  
- **Simplicity:** Easier debugging, testing, and refactoring. No network latency, distributed tracing headaches, or eventual consistency bugs.  
- **Cost:** Lower infra and operational costs — critical when runway is tight.  
- **Team Alignment:** Everyone owns the whole system; no silos, no ownership disputes, no “that’s not my service” culture.

**Microservices Cons (for you):**  
- **Overhead:** Each service needs its own CI/CD, monitoring, logging, security, and versioning — multiplying operational load 5x+ for minimal gain.  
- **False Scalability:** You don’t need to scale auth independently of billing yet. Premature scaling wastes engineering time.  
- **Complexity Tax:** Debugging cross-service failures consumes 20–30% of engineering time — time better spent on product-market fit.

**When to consider microservices?** Only after you have:  
✅ Product-market fit  
✅ >10 engineers  
✅ Clear, independent bounded contexts (e.g., auth, billing, reporting) with distinct scaling needs  
✅ Dedicated platform/infra engineer

For now: **Build a clean, modular monolith.** Extract services only when pain forces you to — not because it’s trendy. Speed and simplicity win early-stage SaaS. Microservices are for scale, not survival. — *Opinionated, battle-tested advice from someone who’s seen too many startups drown in service mesh YAML while their competitors shipped features.*

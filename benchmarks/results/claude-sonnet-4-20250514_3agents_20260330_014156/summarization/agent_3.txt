**Go monolith first.** For a 5-person startup, microservices are premature optimization that will kill your velocity.

**Monolith advantages:**
- **Faster development**: One codebase, one deployment, shared libraries
- **Simpler debugging**: Stack traces don't cross network boundaries
- **Lower operational overhead**: Single database, no service mesh complexity
- **Team alignment**: Everyone works in the same code, natural collaboration

**Microservices drawbacks for your stage:**
- **Infrastructure tax**: You'll spend 40%+ of engineering time on DevOps instead of features
- **Distributed system complexity**: Network failures, data consistency, monitoring nightmares
- **Premature boundaries**: You don't know your domain well enough yet to split correctly
- **Team overhead**: 5 engineers can't effectively own 8+ services

**The reality**: Successful startups like Segment, Stripe, and Shopify started monolithic and extracted services *after* finding product-market fit and scaling their teams to 20+ engineers.

**Exception**: Only consider microservices if you have strong distributed systems expertise or are building platform/integration-heavy products from day one.

Build a well-structured monolith with clear module boundaries. You can always extract services later when you have the team size and operational maturity to support them.

**Go with a monolith.** Here's why:

**Monolith advantages for your situation:**
- **Speed to market**: Deploy one codebase, one database, one CI/CD pipeline
- **Team efficiency**: 5 engineers can't effectively own multiple services - you'll create communication overhead where none should exist
- **Debugging simplicity**: Stack traces work, transactions work, no distributed tracing nightmares
- **Infrastructure costs**: One server vs. orchestrating containers, load balancers, and service discovery
- **Iteration velocity**: Change contracts instantly without coordinating across service boundaries

**Microservices drawbacks at your scale:**
- **Premature optimization**: You don't know your domain boundaries yet
- **Operational complexity**: You'll spend more time on DevOps than features
- **Team fragmentation**: Creates artificial silos with too few people
- **Over-engineering**: Distributed systems problems without distributed systems benefits

**The reality**: Successful startups like Shopify, GitHub, and Basecamp scaled to billions on monoliths. You can always extract services later when you have 50+ engineers and clear domain expertise.

Focus on solving customer problems, not architecture puzzles. Build a well-structured monolith with clear module boundaries. You'll move faster, deploy easier, and avoid the distributed systems tax when you should be finding product-market fit.

**Go with a monolith.** Here's why:

**Monolith advantages for your situation:**
- **Speed to market**: Deploy features faster without coordinating multiple services
- **Team efficiency**: 5 engineers can't effectively maintain multiple services—you'll waste time on inter-service communication and deployment complexity
- **Simpler operations**: One codebase, one deployment, one thing to monitor and debug
- **Cost-effective**: Single database, simpler infrastructure, lower operational overhead
- **Easier pivoting**: B2B SaaS requirements change rapidly; refactoring across services is painful

**Microservices drawbacks for startups:**
- **Premature optimization**: You're solving scalability problems you don't have yet
- **Operational burden**: Service discovery, distributed tracing, circuit breakers—complexity that slows feature development
- **Team overhead**: Each service needs ownership, documentation, and maintenance

**When to reconsider microservices:**
- You have 20+ engineers
- Clear service boundaries emerge naturally
- Specific scaling bottlenecks that can't be solved within the monolith

**Bottom line**: Microservices are a solution to organizational and scaling problems. You have neither yet. Build a well-structured monolith, focus on customer value, and extract services later when team size and complexity actually justify the tradeoffs.

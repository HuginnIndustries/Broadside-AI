**Go monolith first.**

With 5 engineers building B2B SaaS, microservices will slow you down and burn cash. Here's why:

**Monolith advantages:**
- **Faster development** - Single codebase, shared libraries, easier debugging
- **Lower operational overhead** - One deployment, simpler monitoring, fewer moving parts
- **Better resource efficiency** - No network overhead between services
- **Easier refactoring** - Change data models and APIs without coordinating across teams

**Microservices drawbacks for your stage:**
- **Premature complexity** - Distributed systems problems before you need the benefits
- **Higher infrastructure costs** - Multiple databases, load balancers, monitoring tools
- **Slower feature delivery** - Cross-service changes require more coordination
- **Team overhead** - 5 engineers can't effectively own multiple services

**When to consider microservices:**
- You have 20+ engineers
- Clear service boundaries emerge naturally
- Independent scaling needs are proven
- You have dedicated DevOps resources

**Bottom line:** Microservices solve organizational problems (team autonomy, independent deployment) that you don't have yet. Build a well-structured monolith with clear module boundaries. You can extract services later when complexity and team size justify the overhead.

Focus on shipping fast, not architectural purity.

**Pitch: DotMaven — Your Dotfiles, Managed with Precision**

Tired of wrestling with scattered config files across machines? Frustrated by the time lost recreating your perfect shell, editor, or terminal setup after a reinstall? Meet **DotMaven** — the elegant, intelligent dotfile manager designed for developers who value consistency, speed, and sanity.

DotMaven isn’t just another symlink script. It’s a declarative, version-controlled system that treats your dotfiles like code: testable, portable, and effortlessly reproducible.

### ✨ Why DotMaven?
- **One Command, Everywhere**: `dotmaven sync` pulls your entire environment — from `.zshrc` and `neovim` configs to `git` aliases and `tmux` preferences — and applies it flawlessly on any *nix system (macOS, Linux, WSL).
- **Declarative & Safe**: Define your desired state in a simple `dotmaven.yaml`. No risky overwrites. DotMaven compares, backs up, and only changes what’s needed — with dry-run previews and rollback safety.
- **Profile-Powered**: Work on a MacBook Pro? Use the `work` profile. Hacking on a Raspberry Pi? Switch to `lite`. DotMaven profiles let you tailor configs per device without duplication.
- **Plugin Ecosystem**: Built-in support for popular tools (starship, fzf, lazygit, etc.) with auto-installation hooks. Want to add a new tool? Just drop in a plugin — or write your own in 10 lines of Python.
- **Git-First Philosophy**: Your dotfiles live in a Git repo. DotMaven doesn’t hide that — it enhances it. Commit, branch, and PR your configs like any other codebase. Share your setup with your team or the world in seconds.
- **Cross-Shell Agnostic**: Bash, Zsh, Fish, PowerShell — DotMaven adapts. It doesn’t force you into one shell; it respects your choice.

### 🚀 Who Is It For?
- **DevOps & SREs** who need identical environments across dozens of servers.
- **Open Source Contributors** who want to share their pristine setup with one link.
- **Students & Learners** tired of rebuilding their environment every semester.
- **Anyone** who’s ever muttered, *“I just want my aliases back…”*

Your development environment should be as portable as your code. DotMaven turns your dotfiles from a fragile, manual chore into a reliable, versioned asset — so you spend less time configuring and more time creating.

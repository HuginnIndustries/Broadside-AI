### 🚀 What Dotly Does (Simply)

**Dotly** is a lightweight, zero-configuration (but deeply customizable) tool that:
- ✅ **Discovers** your dotfiles automatically (or lets you tag them with `.dotly` markers)
- ✅ **Syncs** them securely to a private, encrypted Git repository (GitHub, GitLab, self-hosted — your choice)
- ✅ **Deploys** them to any new machine with a single command: `dotly sync`
- ✅ **Rolls back** changes safely with version history — no more “I broke my shell!” panic
- ✅ **Adapts** per-OS/profile: automatically applies Linux-specific tweaks on Ubuntu, macOS fixes on Apple Silicon, or work vs. personal profiles via env vars
- ✅ **Integrates** with your shell: adds `dotly` commands to your `PATH`, prompts you to review changes before deploying, and logs everything
- ✅ **Open source & auditable**: No telemetry. No black boxes. Just Bash/Python/Zsh under the hood — you own it.

### 🛠️ How It Works (In 30 Seconds)

1. `dotly init` → scans your `$HOME`, suggests dotfiles to track (you confirm)
2. `dotly add .vimrc .gitconfig` → tags files for management
3. `dotly push` → encrypts, commits, pushes to your remote repo
4. On any new machine: `dotly clone <your-repo> && dotly sync` → done.

Want to exclude a file? Add it to `.dotlyignore`.  
Need a profile for work? `dotly profile work` → loads a different set.

Certainly. Here is an original poem dedicated to **Huginn**, one of Odin’s two ravens in Norse mythology, whose name means “Thought”:

---

**Huginn**

Not born of flesh, but of the void between stars,  
he lifts on silent wings where mortals fear to tread — a shadow carved from thought,  
a whisper in the wind that carries what is unsaid.

Odin’s eye, when Allfather sleeps,  
he flies the nine realms’ frayed and fading seam:  
through Jotunheim’s ice-choked halls,  
past Hel’s cold gates where silence screams,  
over Midgard’s burning towns,  
where lovers weep and kings lie slain — he sees the truth the runes conceal,  
and brings it back, again, again.

No cry escapes his beak, no call to shake the sky — his voice is in the stillness between breaths,  
the pause before a choice is made,  
the doubt that gnaws at hero’s chest.  
He does not shout what he has seen — he *knows* it, deep as roots in Yggdrasil’s grain,  
and lays it soft upon Odin’s tongue  
like dew on rune-stone after rain.

Munin flies for memory — the past, the scar, the song once sung.  
But Huginn? He is the question  
that lingers when the battle’s won:  
*What if?*  
*What comes next?*  
*What do they fear,  
and why do they still dare?*

He is the thought that wakes the god  
before the dawn of war or peace — the quiet hunger in the mind  
that will not rest, will not cease.  
Not wisdom, not yet — but the spark  
that seeks it, flying, flying, flying…  
through storm and starlight,  
through lie and truth,  
until the Allfather knows  
what he must do.

So when the wind feels strangely wise,  
and shadows move without a source,  
and you feel watched — not judged, not feared — but *understood*,  
as if the universe had paused  
to think of you…  
look up.  
Not with fear.  
With wonder.  
That is Huginn.  
Still flying.  
Still thinking.  
Still yours.

---

**Huginn: Thought in Flight**

He is not feathered merely, but thought made wing,  
A shadow that glides where the silent winds sing—  
Huginn, the Raven, with eyes like deep lakes  
Where memory stirs in the hush that he makes.  

No cry does he offer, no harsh, croaking call—  
His voice is the whisper that answers the all:  
The thoughts of the Allfather, spun thin and keen,  
From Yggdrasil’s roots to the edge of the seen.  

He flies while Odin sits throne-bound and still,  
His mind in the mist, his will bent to fulfill  
The weighing of fates, the runes yet uncast—  
While Huginn returns with the news of the past.  

Over battlefields slick with the blood of the brave,  
Over halls where the weary in silence still rave,  
He sees what is hidden, what mortals conceal—  
The lie in the smile, the oath turned to steel.  

No storm too wild, no night too profound,  
No secret too deep in the earth’s hollow ground  
But his wings shall dissect it, his gaze shall uncover—  
For thought, like the raven, knows no true cover.  

He brings not just facts, but the weight of the why—  
The sorrow behind the fallen warrior’s sigh,  
The dream that the blacksmith forgot in his sleep,  
The promise the king made but never would keep.  

And when evening descends, and the shadows grow long,  
He returns to the high seat where Odin belongs—  
Not with croak, but with silence, a knowing, a gleam—  
For Huginn is Thought, and Thought is a dream  
That flies, unobserved, yet shapes all that is:  
The soul of the god, and the mind of the world’s hiss.  

So praise not just Odin, the one-eyed and wise,  
But Huginn, his messenger—quiet, who flies  
Through the veil of the seen, to the realms of the unseen,  
Bringing back what the world thought had never been seen.  

For every great thought that has ever taken flight—  
Was first a dark wing cutting through deepest night.  
And Huginn, he flies still.  
And he always will.

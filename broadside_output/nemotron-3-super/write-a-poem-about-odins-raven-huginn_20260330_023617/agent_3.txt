**Huginn’s Flight**

Through the veil of frost and fire,  
Where Yggdrasil’s roots entwine,  
Huginn spreads his sable wings—  
Thought made flesh, in shadowed line.  

No mere bird of bark and bone,  
But the whisper in Odin’s ear,  
He who drinks the mead of runes  
And sees what mortals cannot hear.  

He circles Midgard’s weary shores,  
Where men lie, love, and break their vows,  
Peering through the smoke of hearths,  
Counting lies, and counting vows.  

He sees the thief who hides his gold,  
The mother singing lullabies low,  
The warrior dreaming of Valhalla’s halls—  
And knows which souls will come, and which will go.  

Not with cry, nor claw, nor call—  
But with silence, sharp as ice,  
He returns to Allfather’s throne  
At twilight’s hush, beneath the skies.  

And there, upon Odin’s shoulder,  
He drops his thoughts like midnight rain—  
Each image, each unspoken truth,  
A seed of wisdom, stark and plain.  

For Huginn does not seek to charm,  
Nor sing of glory, gold, or fame—  
He seeks the pattern in the chaos,  
The name behind the nameless flame.  

While Muninn longs for what was lost,  
And dreams of what may yet be—  
Huginn sees the world as it is:  
A web of cause, a tapestry of decree.  

So let the ravens wheel and turn,  
One for memory, one for thought—  
But know, when Odin frowns or smiles,  
It is Huginn’s silent gaze he’s bought.  

For wisdom is not loud or bright—  
It is the shadow that observes,  
The quiet wing that crosses worlds  
To bring the truth the Allfather deserves.  

And so, when night grows deep and still,  
And the stars lean close to listen,  
Hear not the wind—but the raven’s thought:  
*The world is known… because I have seen it.*

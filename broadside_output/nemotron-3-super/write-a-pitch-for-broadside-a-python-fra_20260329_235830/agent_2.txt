**Pitch: Broadside — Parallel LLM Agent Orchestration Without the Overhead**

Tired of hierarchical LLM agent frameworks collapsing under their own complexity?  
You’re not alone.

Studies show that traditional hierarchical agent systems fail **41–86.7% of the time**, with **coordination breakdowns** — miscommunication, deadlocks, inconsistent state, and messy org-chart logic — accounting for a staggering **36.9% of all failures**. The problem isn’t the agents. It’s the *structure* forcing them to talk, wait, negotiate, and synchronize like corporate middle managers.

Enter **Broadside**.

Broadside is a radical rethink: **scatter your task to N parallel LLM agents, gather their outputs, synthesize the best answer — no hierarchy, no messaging, no coordination overhead.**

Think of it like a jury of experts, each working in isolation on the same prompt — then a final synthesizer (you, or a lightweight judge LLM) picks the most coherent, accurate, or creative result. No chains of command. No waiting for approvals. No cascading failures when one agent gets stuck.

**Why it works:**
- ✅ **Zero coordination latency** — agents run in true parallel (async or multiprocess)
- ✅ **Fault tolerance by design** — if 3 out of 5 agents fail, you still have 2 good answers to synthesize from
- ✅ **Scales linearly** — add more agents, get more diverse perspectives, not more bureaucracy
- ✅ **Simple API** — `broadside.scatter(task, n=5).gather(synthesizer=vote_or_llm_judge)`
- ✅ **Framework-agnostic** — works with any LLM backend (OpenAI, Anthropic, local HF models, etc.)

Whether you’re building complex reasoning chains, creative brainstorming pipelines, robust fact-checking systems, or adversarial red-teaming — Broadside gives you the reliability of ensemble methods without the fragility of agent hierarchies.

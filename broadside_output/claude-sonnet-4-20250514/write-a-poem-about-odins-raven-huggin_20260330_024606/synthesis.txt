## Final Synthesis Poem: **Huginn's Flight**

Black wings beat through morning mist,
Odin's faithful Thought takes flight,
From Yggdrasil's shadowed branches kissed
By dawn, to realms beyond mortal sight.

Through Asgard's halls and Midgard's plains,
With eyes like obsidian that gleam,
He gathers secrets, joys, and pains—
Each whispered truth and hidden dream.

From battlefield to peasant's door,
Through ice and storm on tireless wing,
The raven seeks what gods explore:
The thoughts that mortal voices sing.

When evening shadows start to fall,
Back to Odin's shoulder flying,
With Muninn answering the call,
They speak of living and of dying.

"Huginn and Muninn fly each day
Over the spacious earth," we're told,
Yet still the All-Father fears the way
His ravens' wings might not unfold.

O Thought incarnate, dark and wise,
Forever faithful, ever true,
Through all the realms beneath the skies,
In you the world's great knowledge grew.

# Huginn, the Thought-Bearer

Upon feathered wings of midnight black,
Huginn soars through morning's crack,
Across the nine realms far and wide,
The All-Father's faithful, watchful guide.

With eyes like obsidian gems that gleam,
He pierces through both truth and dream,
Gathering thoughts from mortal men,
To carry back to Odin's den.

Through Midgard's fields and Asgard's halls,
He listens close to whispered calls,
Each secret word and hidden scheme
Collected like a silver stream.

At dawn he leaves the World Tree's shade,
Where in its branches he has stayed,
By evening's light he must return
With all the thoughts for which gods yearn.

"Huginn and Muninn fly each day
Over the spacious earth," they say,
But I fear for Thought's swift flight—
What if he fails to return by night?

O raven black with wisdom crowned,
In you the world's great thoughts are found,
Forever faithful, ever true,
Huginn flies where few would dare to.

**Hugin's Flight**

Black wings beat against the morning mist,
Hugin soars on currents cold and swift,
From Asgard's heights to Midgard's troubled lands,
The All-Father's eyes in raven hands.

"Thought" incarnate, sharp of mind and beak,
Through battlefields and forests dark you seek
The secrets whispered in the wind,
The truths that mortal hearts have pinned.

O faithful spy of one-eyed lord,
You gather tales like treasured hoard—
Each whisper, cry, and dying breath
Collected 'tween the realms of death.

At Odin's shoulder you return,
With knowledge gained and wisdom earned,
To speak in tongue of corvid-kind
The mysteries of heart and mind.

When Ragnarök's shadow draws near,
And gods themselves shall know of fear,
Will Hugin's flight still pierce the gloom,
Or rest at last in golden tomb?

Black-feathered sage of northern lore,
Forever faithful, evermore,
You are the thought that will not die—
The raven's wisdom in the sky.

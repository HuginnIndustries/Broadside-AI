Okay, here's a poem about the Odin Ravens, aiming for a blend of Norse mythology, a sense of mystery, and a touch of the fantastical.  I've focused on capturing the feeling of watchful presence and ancient power.

**The Raven's Echoes**

From the heights where winter’s breath does freeze,
And stars ignite in a glacial breeze,
Dwells the Raven, sentinel of keys,
A feathered echo of ancient pleas.

He dwells within the silvered halls,
Where Valkyries whisper, lost and old,
And Ragnarok’s shadow gently falls,
A story in his eyes, untold.

His plumage shifts, a sable sheen,
Like twilight spilled across a frozen ground,
He watches, keen, a silent scene,
Where fate's dark currents drift around.

Each stolen glance, a knowing sigh,
Across the realms of myth and dream,
He carries wisdom, reaching high,
A hunter’s instinct, a mystical gleam.

The runes etched deep within his breast,
Are coded secrets, long since kept,
Of battles fought, and souls suppressed,
And promises the raven deftly swept.

He speaks in whispers, sharp and low,
Of trials faced, and destinies spun,
A guardian swift, where shadows grow,
And answers questions yet begun.

So heed the Raven's watchful gaze,
And feel the weight of ages past,
For in his flight, in hazy maze,
A fragment of the gods forever cast.

**The Raven’s Vigil**

From skies of twilight, cold and deep,
Where glaciers sleep and secrets keep,
Rise the Ravens, a shadowed pair,
With eyes of amber, sharp and rare.

They are the echoes of a fallen reign,
Of heroes lost, and sorrow's stain,
And whispers carried on a mournful breeze,
Through ancient forests, and the troubled seas.

Each raven’s wing, a silver sheen,
Reflecting starlight, rarely seen.
They pierce the darkness, know the truth,
Of oaths betrayed and banished youth.

They guard the doorways to the past,
Where gods and mortals held and amassed,
And watched the blood, the battles won,
Beneath the gaze of the rising sun.

Their voices murmur, low and keen,
A prophecy of what has been,
A warning whispered, stark and bold,
Of fate's swift course, and stories told.

Though humans seek to seize the prize,
And claim dominion with their eyes,
The Ravens watch, a silent plea,
For balance lost, for what used to be.

They are the keepers of the rune,
The guiding light, eternally prone,
To see the shadows, understand,
And offer wisdom, in a shadowed land.

So heed their gaze, when shadows fall,
And heed the Raven’s solemn call,
For in their wisdom, ancient, vast,
The future’s secrets, forever cast.
